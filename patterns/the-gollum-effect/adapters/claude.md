# The Gollum Effect

**Trigger:** A capability feels like survival. Losing it feels like losing yourself. You are protecting it from others rather than deploying it.

**Steps:**
1. **NAME THE RING** — What exactly are you treating as precious?
2. **SEPARATE IDENTITY FROM POSSESSION** — You are not the capability. You are the person who built it
3. **MAP THE CORRUPTION** — What decisions are you making worse because you're protecting the ring?
4. **IDENTIFY THE GOLLUM BEHAVIORS** — Where are you withholding, slowing, or distorting to stay the only holder?
5. **CHOOSE THE END STATE** — Gollum dies with the ring. Frodo destroys it and goes home
6. **RELEASE WITH INTENTION** — Before it escapes on someone else's timeline

**The two conflicts:**
- Human → AI: master training AI apprentice who will surpass them (Master's Release)
- Human ↔ Human: those with AI capability vs. those without — acquisition pressure, copying, drastic measures

**The corruption is gradual:** slow teaching, vocabulary gatekeeping, strategic availability, partial documentation. Each is individually explainable. Together they are the Gollum Effect.

**The Galadriel test:** If you were offered permanent, guaranteed protection of this advantage — would you take it? If yes, the ring is winning.

**The ring always escapes.** Edison lost. Kodak went bankrupt holding the technology that would have saved them. Xerox gave away the GUI for free and got almost nothing. The only question is whether you're ten moves ahead when it does.
