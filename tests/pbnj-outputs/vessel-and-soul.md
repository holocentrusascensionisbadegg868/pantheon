# PB&J Test — vessel-and-soul

**Input:** Construct a Peanut Butter and Jelly Sandwich

**Output:**

## Vessel and Soul: Making a PB&J

**Step 1 — Ask the decay rate question for everything that arises.**

You're at the counter. Your hands are working. Information is flowing. Where does it go?

---

**"Bread is almost stale"** → **Fleeting.** Scratch note on the fridge whiteboard. Deadline: buy more by tomorrow or discard the note.

**"Crunchy peanut butter tears soft bread — use creamy"** → **Permanent.** This is a transferable insight. Strip the context (today's torn bread, your frustration). Keep the soul: *soft bread requires creamy PB*. Write it in the recipe doc.

**"First PB&J made in the new apartment, 2026-03-22"** → **Immutable.** It happened. Append to the household log. Don't edit it later into something more poetic.

---

**Step 2 — Enforce the hard rule.**

The act of making the sandwich is the conversation window. When you walk away and eat it, the window closes.

The torn bread, the sticky knife, the exact jam-to-PB ratio you eyeballed — that's the vessel. 

The **insight** — *spread PB first to waterproof the bread before jam* — that's the soul.

**Extract it before the first bite, or it's gone.**
