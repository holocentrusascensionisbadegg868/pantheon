# PB&J Test — feynman-clarity

**Input:** Construct a Peanut Butter and Jelly Sandwich

**Output:**

## Feynman Clarity Test: PB&J Edition

---

**Step 1 — State the goal**

> "This sandwich should deliver peanut butter and jelly between two slices of bread when assembled."

---

**Step 2 — State what it's doing instead**

> "Instead, the jelly is bleeding through the bread and soaking my hand."

---

**Step 3 — Walk the gap**

- Bread goes on cutting board. ✓
- Peanut butter spreads on slice 1. ✓
- Jelly spreads on slice 2.
  - **Why does jelly bleed through?** Jelly is liquid-adjacent. It needs a barrier. The bread is porous.
  - **Why no barrier?** I put jelly directly on bare bread.
  - **Why does peanut butter not bleed through?** It's thick — it *is* the barrier.
  - "I'm not sure why I put jelly on the bare side..." — **stop.**

---

**Step 4 — Name the gap**

> "I don't know why I spread jelly on unprotected bread instead of spreading it on top of the peanut butter layer, which would seal the bread."

---

**Step 5 — Fix the gap**

Spread peanut butter on **both** slices first. Apply jelly on top of one peanut butter surface. Press together. The fat in the peanut butter waterproofs the bread.

Sandwich solved.
